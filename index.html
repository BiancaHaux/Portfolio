Portfolio

Projects are collaborations with colleagues from the Centre for eResearch at the University of Auckland and the researchers providing the data. This selection excludes projects containing sensitive and unpublished data and represents only part of my work.

Sea level rise simulation and interactive webpage. Explore sea level changes according to timeline adjustments and the effects of storm events in combination with tide cycles. The 3D model of Auckland was created using Building Footprints, Digital Elevation Models and Imagery combined in Blender. Three.js WebGL rendering was used to create the interactive 3D representation in the browser, the timeline is based on Vis.js. Developed for Associate Professor Giovanni Coco, School of Environment.
Index of Multiple Deprivation, interactive map and automatic report creation for dynamic research analysis. Explore the geography of deprivation and its association with a given health or social outcome over the seven domains. Dynamically create reports for a particular administrative area of interest. Users can select from District Health Boards, Territorial Authorities, or General Electoral Districts. Developed for Associate Professor Dr. Daniel John Exeter, Senior Lecturer, Epidemiology and Biostatistics.
VR visualisation of protein bonding of Human Peroxiredoxin 3, the second most common protein in the human body behind Haemoglobin, using the HTC Vive virtual reality headset. It shows the assembly of monomers into dimers, dimers into rings, and rings into nanotubes. The pH value is adjustable and controls the degree of assembly. This visualisation has been shown at Motat for an exhibition. Developed for Professor Juliet Gerrard, newly appointed the Prime Minister’s Chief Science Advisor.
A browser extension as part of a research project at the University of Auckland monitoring the nature and extent of food advertising through Facebook. AdHealth keeps track of the advertisements people see on Facebook, and determines their AdHealth score based on what types of advertisements they often see in their Newsfeed. The Chrome extension, info website and admin portal are based on HTML and Javascript and integrate with a database. The research project is conducted by Professor Boyd Swinburn and Dr. Stefanie Vandevijvere.
Visualisation of humpback whale migration as they migrate from Raoul Island south to Antarctica. The whales were geotagged / biopsied by Dr. Rochelle Constantine and the research team. The CSV data ranging from October 2015 to March 2016 were plotted on a 3D globe using the Cesium javascript library. Additionally, an open dataset provided by NASA describing the monthly average Antarctic ice coverage was integrated. Developed for Dr. Rochelle Constantine, Senior Lecturer, School of Biological Sciences & Institute of Marine Science.
Alter: Between human and nonhuman is a curatorial project that has a component which is a Virtual Reality exhibition. This exhibition, Alter, foregrounded the works of international artists working within a technoscientific space, teasing out the relations between corporeality and technologies that allow a rethinking of the posthuman. Each artwork was a product of the shifting collaborative process involving artists, engineers, computer scientists, neuroscientists and medical practitioners often incorporating the artist’s corporeality as data or characteristics as part of the technoscientific art work. The physical exhibition was staged at the Gus Fisher Gallery at the University of Auckland in May 2016 and reconfigured as a VR exhibition in July 2017.

Developed for Deborah Lawler-Dormer, Research Manager at Museum of Applied Arts and Sciences, Sydney.
